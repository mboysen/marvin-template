## Lessons learned (full detail in ai-precise/docs/learnings/2026-06-01.md)
1. **The "false positives" were the raw-overlay diagnostic layer, not fires.**
   Grade the system on tracked+fired, not the yellow per-frame layer.
2. **Threshold can't separate overlapping distributions** (0.40 killed everything).
   The lever is temporal persistence (coast + age), not confidence; calibration
   is monotonic and can't separate either.
3. **The static/live gap was the 16:9→square anisotropic squash**, not OOD scale.
   Letterbox fixes it camera-agnostically.
4. **A hard age gate on a memoryless tracker makes misses worse** — coast first.
5. **The live-stream "choppiness/jumpiness" was the GPU clock-lock reverting
   (DVFS) and the 2.4 GHz guest wifi — NOT the model.** Re-lock clocks; record on
   the box from loopback to review (smooth regardless of wifi).
6. **`jetson_clocks` and the camera gate** bit twice: clocks revert (not
   persisted); the app unit won't start without `/dev/ai-precise-cam`.
7. **Build where the igpu base is cached** (mn-demo), keep code-of-record on
   sparky `main`. Loopback `record-test.py` + resumable `rsync` is the field-capture
   workflow that beats spotty wifi.
